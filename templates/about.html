problem of garbage classification

Manual garbage classification heavily relies on human sorting, which is prone to errors. Mistakes can occur due to fatigue, inconsistency, or lack of expertiseSorting garbage manually is a time-consuming process, especially when dealing with large quantities of waste. It requires significant manpower and resources, which can result in delays and inefficiencies in waste management operations.Sorting waste manually can expose workers to potential health and safety hazards. Certain types of waste may be toxic, sharp, or contaminated, posing risks to workers' well-being. Manual garbage classification is challenging to scale up to handle increasing waste volumes. As waste production grows, it becomes increasingly difficult to manage and sort garbage efficiently without automated systems.
